LEGAL PRINCIPLES OVERVIEW

1. Contract Law

A contract is a legally binding agreement between two or more parties. 
For a contract to be valid, the following elements must be present:

- Offer: One party proposes terms.
- Acceptance: The other party agrees to the terms.
- Consideration: Something of value is exchanged.
- Intention: Both parties intend to create legal obligations.

If any of these elements are missing, the contract may be considered invalid.

2. Breach of Contract

A breach of contract occurs when one party fails to perform their contractual obligations.

Common remedies include:

- Damages
- Specific performance
- Contract termination

3. Privacy Law

Privacy law regulates how personal information is collected and used.

Personal data refers to information relating to an identifiable individual.

Examples include:

- name
- email address
- identification number
- location data

Organizations must ensure that personal data is processed lawfully and securely.

4. Intellectual Property

Intellectual property refers to creations of the mind.

The main types include:

- Copyright
- Trademark
- Patent
- Trade secrets

Patents protect inventions and typically last for a limited period, usually 20 years.

5. Legal Liability

Legal liability arises when a person or organization is legally responsible for harm or damages.

Liability may arise in situations such as:

- negligence
- breach of duty
- violation of statutory obligations
